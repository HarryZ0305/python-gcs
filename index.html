What's on the glass

Everything you need on one screen.

The features that ship in v1 — pulled straight from the build, not a wishlist.

01

Live telemetry gauges

Altitude, speed, battery, and GPS strength on sweeping arc gauges — readable at a glance from across the field.

02

Maps that work offline

Plan on a real map, then cache the area to disk before you drive out — no signal needed once you're standing in the field.

03

3D attitude view

A live model that banks and pitches with the aircraft, so you can verify orientation before you ever leave the ground.

04

Mission planning

Drop waypoints with takeoff and landing, upload to the vehicle, and save or load full missions as files for next time.

05

Keyboard flight

Take manual offboard control with the keyboard to nudge the aircraft and bench-test attitude response in the simulator.

06

Pre-flight safety checks

A live checklist and alert panel that flags GPS, battery, and link issues — and tells you exactly what's blocking arming.

Built for real iron

Bench-tested against the gear it's made for.

Python GCS is developed against PX4 SITL and targets a Holybro S500 quad running a Pixhawk 6C. If you fly the same setup, it's built for your bird. If you fly something else PX4-based, it speaks plain MAVLink — give it a try.
